## Executive Summary

As of March 19, 2026, Rocket Lab trades at approximately **$72/share** — near its post-ATM-announcement trough, and roughly 28% below its all-time high of $99.58 reached on January 16, 2026. The current price implies near-zero embedded value for Neutron, effectively pricing the stock as an Electron launch + space systems company with a free option on a medium-lift rocket.

The investment thesis is a staged binary: each hardware milestone Neutron clears expands the addressable market and re-rates the stock meaningfully. The single critical gate remaining is stage 1 tank requalification — everything else in the Q4 2026 launch plan is either complete or on schedule.

## Conclusion

RKLB at $72 is priced as an Electron-only business with a free option on Neutron. The tank requalification is the only variable between the current price and an $82–95 re-rating. The maximum asymmetric moment is the static fire (S3) — if Archimedes passes full-duration testing, the stock should re-rate toward $95–115 before Flight 1 occurs, as the market will begin pricing the orbit success probability at 60–70%.

The bear case ($38–48) requires two hardware failures in a row — plausible but historically atypical for Rocket Lab, which has achieved 21 successful Electron missions in 2025 alone, reflecting deep manufacturing and test maturity.

**For a long-term investor:** The current price offers the best risk/reward since the post-SPAC period. The $1.85B in confirmed backlog provides fundamental support; Neutron is the optionality layer on top.
